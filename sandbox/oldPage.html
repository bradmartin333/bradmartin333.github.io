Bitmap Software AutoFocus

Apr 10, 2021

I have been working on this concept of autofocusing based on the red channel of pixel colors within a Bitmap. The core method takes pixels from nodes on a grid, then looks at the % difference between that node's red value and its neighbors up to the next node. High % diff means a lot of edges are present and that is consistent with being more in focus. The next big development was sampling tiles of a grid within the image and only running the % difference method for tiles that contained high entropy. I use the entropy calc from MathNet Numerics all the time. It is very good at picking out regions of interest within a pixel array, and researchers agree. The results were better than I expected! The next step was implementing this method on the tools at work. The camera Z stage steps around and frames are grabbed and scored, resulting in a best Z position for best focus. It works great and is a lot easier than forcing the user to set a region of interest on their own. The idea came from digital camera autofocus, as seen in the pictures here. I still have to take my improvements from the work repo (Adding parameters to customize the grid and scoring) and add them to the RedFocus repo.
